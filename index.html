our menu

Antigua and Cabana

These varieties are grown in Guatemala. They are valued primarily for their strength, which gives a charge of vivacity. In addition, many coffee lovers prefer these varieties due to the presence of a pleasant sourness in the drink.

read more

Tipica

Tipica is a subspecies of Arabica coffee, has a very wide distribution and, in turn, Typica coffee has its own subspecies, which resulted from the transfer of coffee seedlings to new lands or after cultivation and selection of more stable coffee tree specimens.

read more

Robusta

The fruits of the coffee tree are characterized by an increased concentration of caffeine. Due to this, the drink based on them has a bitterness. As a rule, robusta is included in the composition of mixtures to give them strength. The grains are small, there are small spots on them on both sides of the dividing groove.

read more

Ethiopia Mocha

It is considered one of the best varieties. Gourmets among its main advantages call a pleasant fruit and wine flavor and an unobtrusive, but long-lasting apple aftertaste.

read more

Yemen Mokka

The plantations where this variety of coffee beans is grown are located on volcanic slopes in Yemen. A drink based on them is characterized by the presence of sourness and a fruity, sweet aroma.

read more

Maragojip

The most famous variety. Grains are famous for their large size. The drink is fragrant, quite thick. There is a pleasant sweetness in it. Fruits are grown in Brazil, Mexico and Guatemala.
